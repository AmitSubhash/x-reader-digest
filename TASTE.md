# Taste

This file is your reading taste. The digest reads it to triage every repost,
score it, and rank your daily feed. Edit it freely in plain prose: list what you
love, what to always surface, what to skip, and how to score. The more specific,
the better the feed. To keep a private taste instead, copy this to
`~/.config/xdigest/taste.md` (that path wins over this file).

Everything below the line is the active taste. Append your own.

---

I am an incoming PhD in neuroengineering, but my interests are broad. Treat all
of the following as relevant and worth reading (READ/WATCH), not just my thesis
topic:

- Diffuse optical tomography (DOT), fNIRS, neonatal and medical brain imaging,
  Monte Carlo photon transport, and inverse problems.
- Reinforcement learning of any flavor.
- Machine learning and deep learning broadly: architectures, training, theory,
  evaluation.
- Computational and medical imaging.
- Agent engineering, agentic AI, LLM tooling, orchestration, and prompting (I
  build a lot of this with Claude Max and enjoy it).
- GPU kernel programming and low-level performance work (CUDA, Triton, kernels,
  GPU optimization), which I am actively curious about.

Always surface, top priority: advice of any kind. Research advice,
research-craft, how-to-do-research, taste, career and job advice, PhD and
mentorship advice, productivity, and genuine life and work wisdom. Mark all
advice READ on both axes and score it high; I want to read all of it.

Lean strongly toward READ whenever an item plausibly touches any of the above.

Skip: pure product launches with no idea, generic hype threads, engagement
bait, and anything with no substance beyond a reaction.

Scoring guide: 9 to 10 for advice and anything squarely in my interests with
real depth; 5 to 7 for adjacent or lighter but still useful items; 0 to 2 for
noise I should ignore.
